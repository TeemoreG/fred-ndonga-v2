Why Your Catering Business Is Losing Money

April 28, 2025 7 min read 2.7k views

You're booking events. Your food is good. But at the end of the month, there's nothing left. Here's why — and how to fix it.

The Harsh Truth

Most catering businesses are profitable on paper but losing money in reality. The difference is knowing your real costs — not just what you think they are.

The 5 Hidden Money Drains in Catering

1. You're Underpricing Your Events

Most caterers price based on what competitors charge, not on their actual costs. Calculate your true cost per event: ingredients + labor + transport + equipment + overhead + profit margin. If you're not charging at least 30% above cost, you're losing money.

Example: If an event costs you KES 50,000, you should charge at least KES 65,000 for a 30% margin.

2. Labor Is Eating Your Profits

How many staff do you send to an event? Most caterers over-staff by 20-30%. Track exactly how many people you need for different event sizes. Create a staffing matrix: 50 guests = X staff. 100 guests = Y staff.

3. Food Waste Is Invisible

Every event has leftovers. Track what comes back uneaten. Adjust your portions accordingly. One catering company I worked with reduced food waste by 25% just by serving 10% smaller portions — and guests never noticed.

4. Transport Costs Are Bleeding You

Fuel, vehicle maintenance, driver time — these add up quickly. Batch your deliveries when possible. Charge a separate transport fee for distant locations.

5. You're Not Charging for Extras

Late-night service? Extra hour? Special equipment? These should be billed separately, not absorbed into the base price. Create a clear "extras" menu that clients can add on.
