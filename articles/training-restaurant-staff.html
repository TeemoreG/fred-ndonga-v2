Training Your Restaurant Staff: A Practical Guide

May 10, 2025 7 min read 1.8k views

Your food can be world-class. Your ambiance can be perfect. But if your staff delivers poor service, guests won't return.

After training hundreds of restaurant staff across Kenya, here's what actually works — not theory, but practical systems you can implement tomorrow.

❌ The Problem with Most Training Programs

Most restaurants do one of two things: they either throw new staff into the deep end with zero training, or they sit them in a classroom for 8 hours reading a manual. Both fail.

Classroom training without floor practice = forgotten within a week.

No training at all = chaos, complaints, and turnover.

📅 The 5-Day Onboarding System

1
Day 1: Shadow & Observe
New staff shadow your best server for a full shift. They watch, take notes, ask questions — no touching customers. End of shift: review the menu together.
2
Day 2: The Menu Exam
Test them on menu knowledge (ingredients, allergens, recommendations). Practice taking mock orders. Role-play common customer questions.
3
Day 3: Floor Practice with Supervision
They take tables while being observed by a senior server. No correction in front of guests — coach after. Focus on order accuracy and greeting protocol.
4
Day 4: Independent with Check-Ins
New staff works a full shift independently. Manager checks in every hour. End-of-shift debrief: what went well? what needs work?
5
Day 5: Final Assessment
Work another full shift. Manager observes and scores on greeting, order taking, upselling, problem-solving. Pass = certified. Fail = repeat days 3-4.

⚡ Weekly Drills That Take 15 Minutes

Training isn't a one-time event. It's a habit.

  • Monday: Menu quiz (change one item each week)
  • Wednesday: Role-play a difficult customer scenario
  • Friday: Review the week's guest feedback
💡 The Golden Rule of Restaurant Training
Train for the worst, not the best. Don't just train for a perfect shift. Train staff how to handle: an angry customer, a wrong order, a spill on a guest, running out of an item, a credit card that won't work.

✅ Signs Your Training is Working

  • New staff are confident within 2 weeks
  • Guest compliments mention specific staff by name
  • Complaints about service drop
  • Staff turnover decreases
🎯 Your next step: Pick one drill from this guide and do it this week. Start small. Build momentum.
