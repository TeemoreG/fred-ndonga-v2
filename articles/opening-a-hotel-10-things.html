Opening a Hotel? 10 Things I Wish I Knew

May 3, 2025 10 min read 3.1k views

I've consulted on hotel openings across Kenya. And I've seen the same mistakes — over and over. Here's what separates a smooth opening from a disaster.

Quick Reality Check

Opening a hotel is 10x harder than running an existing one. Every system is untested. Every staff member is new. Every guest is your first impression. Plan accordingly.

1. Start Training 60 Days Before Opening

Most owners start training the week before opening. That's a catastrophe. Your staff needs time to practice, make mistakes, and build confidence. Start training two months out, even if there are no guests.

Example: Run mock check-ins, mock breakfast service, mock complaint handling — all before day one.

2. Your First 30 Days Will Be Chaos. Plan For It.

Expect things to go wrong. Expect systems to fail. The question isn't "will there be problems?" but "how will you handle them?" Have a clear escalation path for every department.

3. Don't Open at 100% Capacity

Open at 50% occupancy for the first 2 weeks. Let your team learn without being overwhelmed. Gradually increase to 75%, then 100%. Your reviews will thank you.

4. Your SOPs Will Change Immediately — That's Fine

Write your standard operating procedures before opening. But know that within the first month, you'll rewrite half of them. That's normal. Don't be precious about your plans.

Front Desk SOP Checklist:

  • Greeting protocol (within 10 seconds)
  • Check-in process (under 3 minutes)
  • Payment handling procedure
  • Room assignment guidelines
  • Guest information privacy
  • Check-out and farewell

5. Hire for Attitude, Train for Skill

You can teach someone to use your booking system. You can't teach them to smile under pressure. Hire people who care. Train the rest.

6. Test Every System With Real People

Invite friends and family for "soft opening" nights. Give them free meals in exchange for honest feedback. Watch where bottlenecks happen. Fix them before paying guests arrive.

7. Your First Review Will Be Brutal. Don't Panic.

Someone will write a bad review in your first month. It's almost guaranteed. How you respond matters more than the review itself. Apologize publicly. Fix the issue privately. Move on.

8. Budget Double for Your Launch Marketing

Everyone underestimates how much it costs to attract those first guests. Plan for 2x your original marketing budget. You'll need it.

9. Empower Your Front Desk to Make Decisions

When a guest has a problem, your front desk shouldn't need manager approval for every small fix. Give them authority to offer discounts or freebies up to a certain amount. Speed matters.

10. Have a 90-Day Post-Opening Plan

Opening day isn't the finish line — it's the starting line. Plan your support, training, and system improvements for the first 90 days. That's when most hotels stumble.
