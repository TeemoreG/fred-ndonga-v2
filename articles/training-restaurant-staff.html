Training Your Restaurant Staff: A Practical Guide

May 28, 2026 7 min read 1.8k views

Your food can be world-class. Your ambiance can be perfect. But if your staff delivers poor service, guests won't return.

After training hundreds of restaurant staff across Kenya — from small cafes to large hotel restaurants — we've refined a system that actually works. Here's what we've learned, not theory, but practical systems you can implement tomorrow.

"We don't believe in classroom training without floor practice. That's forgotten within a week."

❌ The Problem with Most Training Programs

Most restaurants do one of two things: they either throw new staff into the deep end with zero training, or they sit them in a classroom for 8 hours reading a manual. Both fail.

Classroom training without floor practice = forgotten within a week.

No training at all = chaos, complaints, and turnover (which costs you 150% of their salary).

💡 The Prime Consultancy Approach:
Training should be 20% classroom, 80% floor practice. We learned this from years of running Trainer of Trainers programs at Sarova Hotels.

📅 The 5-Day Onboarding System

This is the exact system we use when training new teams for our clients. It works for everything from fine dining to fast casual.

1
Day 1: Shadow & Observe
New staff shadow your best server for a full shift. They watch, take notes, ask questions — no touching customers. End of shift: review the menu together. We also review our service standards checklist.
2
Day 2: The Menu Exam
Test them on menu knowledge (ingredients, allergens, preparation methods, recommendations). Practice taking mock orders. Role-play common customer questions and objections.
3
Day 3: Floor Practice with Supervision
They take tables while being observed by a senior server or manager. No correction in front of guests — coach after. Focus on greeting protocol, order accuracy, and upselling technique.
4
Day 4: Independent with Check-Ins
New staff works a full shift independently. Manager checks in every hour. End-of-shift debrief: what went well? what needs work? Document both.
5
Day 5: Final Assessment
Work another full shift. Manager observes and scores on: greeting (10 pts), order taking (20 pts), upselling (10 pts), accuracy (30 pts), problem-solving (20 pts), teamwork (10 pts). Pass (80%+) = certified. Fail = repeat days 3-4.

⚡ Weekly Drills That Take 15 Minutes

Training isn't a one-time event. It's a habit. Here's what we recommend for ongoing development:

  • Monday (10 min): Menu quiz — change one item each week to keep it fresh
  • Wednesday (15 min): Role-play a difficult customer scenario (the angry guest, the indecisive diner, the food allergy question)
  • Friday (10 min): Review the week's guest feedback — what went well? what can we improve?
💡 The Golden Rule of Restaurant Training
Train for the worst, not the best. Don't just train for a perfect shift. Train staff how to handle: an angry customer, a wrong order, a spill on a guest, running out of an item, a credit card that won't work, a customer with a food allergy. These moments define your reputation.

✅ Signs Your Training is Working

  • New staff are confident and independent within 2 weeks
  • Guest compliments mention specific staff by name
  • Service-related complaints drop by 40% or more
  • Staff turnover decreases significantly (trained staff stay)
  • Your best servers become your trainers (multiplying your impact)
