Why Your Catering Business Is Losing Money

May 18, 2026 7 min read 2.7k views

You're booking events. Your food is good. But at the end of the month, there's nothing left. Here's why — and how to fix it, based on our experience auditing catering businesses across Kenya.

The Harsh Truth

Most catering businesses are profitable on paper but losing money in reality. The difference is knowing your real costs — not just what you think they are. We've seen this pattern in over 85 establishments.

"If you don't know your cost per event within 10%, you're likely losing money on every booking."

The 5 Hidden Money Drains in Catering

1. You're Underpricing Your Events

Most caterers price based on what competitors charge, not on their actual costs. Calculate your true cost per event: ingredients + labor + transport + equipment + overhead + profit margin. If you're not charging at least 30% above cost, you're losing money.

Example from our client work: An event costing KES 50,000 should be priced at least KES 65,000 for a 30% margin. Most caterers we audit are at 15-20%.

2. Labor Is Eating Your Profits

How many staff do you send to an event? Most caterers over-staff by 20-30%. Track exactly how many people you need for different event sizes. Create a staffing matrix: 50 guests = X staff. 100 guests = Y staff. We helped one client reduce labor costs by 25% just by optimizing their crew schedules.

3. Food Waste Is Invisible

Every event has leftovers. Track what comes back uneaten. Adjust your portions accordingly. One catering company we worked with reduced food waste by 25% just by serving 10% smaller portions — and guests never noticed. That's pure profit back in your pocket.

4. Transport Costs Are Bleeding You

Fuel, vehicle maintenance, driver time — these add up quickly. Batch your deliveries when possible. Charge a separate transport fee for distant locations. Many caterers we audit are losing 5-10% of revenue on unbilled transport.

5. You're Not Charging for Extras

Late-night service? Extra hour? Special equipment? These should be billed separately, not absorbed into the base price. Create a clear "extras" menu that clients can add on. This alone added 15% to one client's bottom line.
